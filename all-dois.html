🝀 VARZIN – DOI Archive Status

Updated following independent adversarial audit · July 2026

Status update (July 2026): Following an independent, multi-stage adversarial audit of the VARZIN computational core (see VARZIN-COMPUTATIONAL-AUDIT-CLOSURE-v1) and a broader review of how these records were produced, the author has requested voluntary withdrawal of the 29 records previously listed on this page (19 Zenodo, 10 OSF) via each platform's formal withdrawal process. Withdrawn records are retained by Zenodo/OSF as "tombstone" pages showing the original citation metadata and the reason for withdrawal — they are not erased from the scholarly record, only marked as retracted by the author's own request. This page will be updated with the final tombstone links once each platform confirms processing.

What remains active

One preprint is retained, as its core claims (LUXVAR phonotactic structure and morpheme-family statistics) were independently re-verified and are not affected by the audit finding above:

10.5281/zenodo.20691858 — "LUXVAR: A Constructed Language with Distinctive Morphemic Structure and AI-Recoverable Morpheme Families"

Note: within this same paper, the phonotactic/morphemic findings are solidly established; the "AI-recoverable" claim in the title (inter-model ARI=0.812) remains preliminary and is the specific subject of the computational-core audit above — see the Results table on the Cyclical Resonance report for full status.
