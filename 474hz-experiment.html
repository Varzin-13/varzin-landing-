Frequency × Cognition

This experiment measures whether specific audio frequencies produce measurable differences in cognitive performance. You will be assigned to one of four conditions — you will not know which frequency (if any) you hear.

Scientific Design:
✓ Blind: You do not know your frequency condition
✓ Controlled: 4 conditions: 474Hz · 432Hz · 528Hz · Silence
✓ Measurable: Reaction time · Word association latency · Pattern accuracy · Memory recall
→ Pre-registered hypotheses — results are valid regardless of outcome
→ Volume must be fixed before starting and not changed during experiment

Thank you for participating.
Please send your raw data to contact@varzin.org with subject line "474Hz Experiment Result".
Your anonymous data will be aggregated with other participants to test whether 474Hz produces measurable cognitive differences vs other conditions.
What your data contributes:
When we collect ≥10 participants per condition (40 total), we can run:
· ANOVA: condition effect on reaction time
· Kruskal-Wallis: non-parametric comparison
· Effect size (Cohen's d): 474Hz vs 432Hz vs 528Hz vs silence
· If 474Hz shows p<0.05 with d>0.5 → first empirical evidence
· If not → architectural role only (equally important finding)
