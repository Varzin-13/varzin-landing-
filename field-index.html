Overview

This field index is the visual companion to the project’s evidence-first public record. It is meant to make the structure of the project easier to inspect, not to elevate unsupported claims through interface design.

In the current version of VARZIN, three questions remain active:

  • Which affine and finite-state results are formally valid?
  • Does LUXVAR contain reproducible designed structure under controlled analysis?
  • Can any of that structure be detected in preregistered human or model-based tests?

Claims about consciousness fields, frequency-to-meaning causation, historical resonance, or external memory remain outside the active evidential core unless reintroduced under new independent protocols.

Affine Memory Group \(Aff(\mathbb{Z}_N)\)

The most durable mathematical core of the project lies in the affine family over finite cyclic spaces. For \(N=12\), valid operators are exactly those with \(\gcd(a,12)=1\), which yields four multiplier classes \(a \in \{1,5,7,11\}\), each with 12 translations.

\[ A_{a,b}(g) = (a g + b) \bmod N, \qquad A_{a,b}\text{ bijective iff }\gcd(a,N)=1 \] \[ |Aff(\mathbb{Z}_N)| = N\varphi(N), \qquad |Aff(\mathbb{Z}_{12})| = 12\cdot 4 = 48 \]

In the public framing of the project, this result stands independently of later symbolic, historical, or field-based interpretations.

Mirror-13 in Context

Mirror-13 is best presented as a conditional computational object linked to the affine family and augmented state transitions. The public record may keep the reported MDL result, but it should clearly state that simplicity or compactness is not equivalent to cognition, semantics, or external memory.

\[ M^*(g,\phi,p) = ((N-g) \bmod N,\ g,\ -p) \]

Current public interpretation should remain narrow:

  • Mirror-13 is a reported minimal anchor under a specific computational specification.
  • It is not logically necessary for every LUXVAR-related interpretation.
  • It is not evidence of a hidden field, external memory, or independent semantic origin.

Recall Formula and POT Suite

The recall layer is one of the more useful reframings in the later project. Publicly, it should be described as a structured hypothesis about reconstruction under limited observation, not as evidence for external contact or hidden cognition.

\[ Recall = Structure\ Match + Prior + Hypothesis\ Space + Observation \]

Interpretive reading

  • Structure Match: the candidate model must fit the underlying rule class.
  • Prior: correct priors accelerate recovery; wrong priors can destroy performance.
  • Hypothesis Space: recovery depends on whether the right family of explanations is even available.
  • Observation: limited evidence constrains how quickly the right structure can be identified.

This is scientifically useful as a reasoning framework. It does not, by itself, establish external memory or a field-like source of recall.

1.3B Morphological Scan — Public Reading

The large synthetic corpus analysis should be presented with caution. It can reveal properties of generator design, clustering tendencies, and leakage, but synthetic scale alone must not be mistaken for empirical validation.

Natural morphology

k = 3 is the strongest reported elbow. The public reading should be conservative: the intended Hexacore or five/six-way morphology was not confirmed by unsupervised structure alone.

Root associations

V ≈ 0.003 indicates negligible association between roots and symbolic frequency labels or field labels in the tested computational setting.

Leakage finding

The project itself reports that graph leakage can create strong apparent signals. This is an important strength of the revised framing because it treats circularity as a risk, not a hidden detail.

Interpretive boundary: interval detection and enrichment signals in a synthetic corpus describe the generator or construction pipeline unless shown otherwise. They are not public evidence of a field phenomenon.

LUXVAR as a Symbolic Bridge

LUXVAR is best presented here as a constructed symbolic lexicon with reported structural regularities. The evidence-first framing should avoid presenting it as a validated natural language, a field-derived ontology, or an independently established semantic system.

What can be said carefully

  • Some non-random morphology-like regularities were reported.
  • Some grouping results across models were reported on Core-30.
  • The best affine bridge reported for LUXVAR was shift family \(a=1\), not Mirror-13.
  • Frequency encoding was tested and not supported.

What should not be claimed here

  • Independent semantic emergence as a settled fact.
  • Field interaction or external memory.
  • Universal or non-human origin.
  • Frequency-to-meaning causation.

M-02 and ETFM Public Interpretation

M-02 semantic field function

M-02 can remain on the site as a formal or simulated model layer, but it should be explicitly labeled as a mathematical abstraction whose parameters require empirical justification before stronger interpretation.

\[ S(w,m,\tau)= C \cdot e^{i(\omega\tau + kw)} \cdot \Sigma(m) \cdot F(w,m) \cdot TIC(\tau) \]
  • \(C\): normalization or calibration constant
  • \(\Sigma(m)\): semantic similarity summary under a chosen representation
  • \(F(w,m)\): evaluator agreement term
  • \(TIC(\tau)\): temporal consistency or decay term

Without independent empirical grounding, this should remain a modeling layer, not a field claim.

Archive Boundary

Earlier versions of the project contained broader symbolic, cosmological, and field-oriented layers. Those materials may remain available as historical archive, but they should not be mixed with current evidence statements unless reintroduced under new preregistered and independently testable protocols.

  • Keep them as historical records, not active evidence.
  • Label symbolic frequencies as archival unless empirically supported.
  • Keep ETFM in visualization territory unless a new protocol changes its status.
  • Keep external-memory claims out of the validated core until independent tests succeed.
